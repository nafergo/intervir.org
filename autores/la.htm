Lawrence Auld

 

 

   O Dr. Lawrence Auld é professor associado na School of Computer Science and Communication, Department of Communication and Broadcasting, na East Carolina University, Greenville, Carolina do Norte, EUA. Consultor a tempo inteiro, trabalha com alunos na comunicação e na educação do ensino básico. É também co-director do Virtual Reality and Education Laboratory, co-editor da revistaVR in the Schools, e lecciona uma disciplina por semestre.

   O Dr. Auld fez o seu doutoramento em Library and Information Studies, com um ênfase especial nos meios visuais. O seu Mestrado é na área dos serviços de biblioteca. As suas publicações incluem numerosos artigos em revistas e vários livros. Ensina a todas as suas turmas através da Internet.

   O Dr. Auld começou a trabalhar com computadores em 1962, quando era Director of Centralized Processing, na Biblioteca Pública do Havai. Posteriormente, esteve envolvido em projectos de informatização de bibliotecas, na Biblioteca Universitária de Oakland (Rochester, Michigan) e na Biblioteca da Universidade do Estado de Oregon. Publicou dois livros sobre a aplicação de folhas de cálculo em bibliotecas. Mais recentemente, orientou os seus interesses informáticos na direcção da realidade virtual. Várias das publicações do Dr. Auld sobre a utilização da realidade virtual na educação podem ser encontradas na página web do Virtual Reality and Education Laboratory.

   Os outros interesses do Dr. Auld incluem fotografia e arte, carpintaria e jardinagem.

 

   Dr. Lawrence Auld is an associate professor in the School of Computer Science and Communication, Department of Communication and Broadcasting, at East Carolina University, Greenville, North Carolina, USA. He is a full-time advisor, working with students in communication and in elementary and middle-grades education. He is also co-director of the Virtual Reality and Education Laboratory, co-editor of the refereed journal VR in the Schools, and teaches one course per semester.

   Dr. Auld's Ph.D. is in Library and Information Studies, with a strong emphasis on visual media. His master's degree is in library service. His publications include numerous journal articles and several books. He teaches all of his classes online.

   Dr. Auld began working with computers in 1962 while he was the Director of Centralized Processing, State Library of Hawaii. He was then involved with library automation projects at Oakland University Library (Rochester, Michigan) and Oregon State University Library. He published two books on library applications of spreadsheets. More recently he has directed his computer interests toward virtual reality. A number of Dr. Auld's publications on the use of virtual reality in education can be found at the Virtual Reality and Education Laboratory.

   Dr. Auld's other interests include photography and art, woodworking, and gardening.
