Brent Wilson

 

     Brent G. Wilson é Bacharel em Ciências e Psicologia Com Louvor e Distinção,  desde Dezembro de 1976 pela Universidade de Brigham Young e Doutor em Filosofia, Ciência  e Tecnologia Educativa pela mesma Universidade, desde Dezembro de 1982. Foi Professor Assistente de Tecnologias Educativas na Universidade de Northern Illinois, onde fez programas para computação e design educativos, entre 1982 e 1987. Em 1987, ingressou na Universidade de Colorado em Denver onde foi Professor Associado de Tecnologias da Informação e Aprendizagem (ILT), até 1999.

     Actualmente é Professor e Coordenador do Programa de Tecnologias da Informação e Aprendizagem na mesma Universidade.

     Tem colaborado com inúmeros artigos e capítulos em revistas e livros da especialidade.  É autor e co-autor de várias  obras na área das Novas Tecnologias do Ensino, tais como, “Constructivist learning environments: Case studies in instructional design (1996)”, “Learning with technology in the classroom: A constructivist perspective” (1999), “Technology making a difference: The Peakview Elementary School Study” (1994) e “Building expert systems for education and training”  (1990).

     Brent G. Wilson is Bachelor of Science cum laude, Psychology, in Brigham Young University, since December 1976 and Doctor of Philosophy, Instructional Science and Technology, in the same University, since December 1982. He was Assistant Professor of Instructional Technology in Northern Illinois University where he established programs in instructional computing and instructional design, between 1982 and 1987.  In 1987, he entered the University of Colorado at Denver as an Associate Professor of Information and Learning Technologies (ILT), until 1999.

     Presently Brent Wilson is Professor of Information and Learning Technologies Program Coordinator, at the University of Colorado at Denver.

     He worked in many peer-reviewed articles and chapters in specialised magazines and books. He was author and co-author of several books dealing with the New Technologies in Teaching and Learning, such as, “Constructivist learning environments: Case studies in instructional design (1996)”, “Learning with technology in the classroom: A constructivist perspective (1999)”, “Technology making a difference: The Peakview Elementary School Study (1994)” and “Building expert systems for education and training  (1990)”.
