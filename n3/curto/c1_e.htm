ICT Competence Centre in São Tomé and Principe[1]

João Paulo Curto

 

INTRODUCTION

     When I was chosen to open a Competence Centre in Information and Communication Technology in São Tomé and Principe (STP), I knew nothing consistent about this country. The vague information I had coincided in one point: it was a country with great structural problems and a low level of education and qualification.

     Still in Portugal I had a pleasant surprise: this country had an online daily journal, “Tela Non” (http://www.cstome.net/diario/index.htm) which I became a frequent reader of and it allowed myself to get acquainted with some of its political, economic and social aspects. To obtain more information I had to resort to Internet search but the information I got was generally very poor. It was easy for me to conclude that this was an area to be developed in the project context.

     The search for published material about STP was very difficult since I only found a book on STP in one bookshop, from the three best-furnished bookshops of Lisbon.

 

THE COMPETENCE CENTRE

 Genesis

     Financed by the Portuguese Ministry of Education through the European Affairs and International Relations Office (GAERI) and with the support of Programa Nónio, the Competence Centre resulted from a protocol between Portugal and the Democratic Republic of São Tomé and Principe, and signed by the respective prime ministers in 1999. This Centre is included in a broader set of measures, concerning the support to the Educational System Reform in São Tomé and Principe.

     The Portuguese Cooperation Institute and the Polytechnic Institute of São Tomé and Principe are also supporting this project, through the official Dispatch # 03/GMEJC/2001.

 

Objectives

     After a short period of time for the installation of its resources[2] - 2 weeks - to which contributed the collaboration and support, both of the local educative authorities, namely, the Ministry of Education and the Higher Polytechnic Institute, and the importer, the Competence Centre formally began its activity on 13th February, 2001, with four major well defined objectives:

1. Educate, divulge and making teachers aware of ICT tools, aiming at an increasing integration of those tools in Education;

2.  Support future activities and projects, in ICT area, in an educational context;

3. Support the implementation and development of cooperative projects, under the responsibility of the Portuguese Ministry of Education, in collaboration with Institutional Entities of São Tomé;

4. Establishing partnerships with private sectors and entities, national or foreign, preferably related to education and training.

 

Training

     Installed in the Higher Polytechnic Institute, the only university teaching unit in the country, its teachers and students are the priority public in the development of training courses in ICT. In the 2nd semester of 2001 the Centre carried out two short duration courses for the teaching staff (planned according to a survey to estimate their needs), and weekly training throughout the semester for all 2nd and 3rd term (final term) classes, included in the curricular planning.

     In the academic year 2001/2002, the training courses carried out by this Centre are covering 70 students (87,5%) of 1st term, 19 students (50%) 2nd term and 10 students (100%) 3rd term. Thus, the Centre is responsible for the training in ICT of 99 students, which represents 76% of the total students in the institute (excluding 0 term students who are not included in this type of training). In each semester one more training level is launched, to widen, in a growing and comprehensive way, the competence of students in ICT, and whose level will stabilize in the 2nd semester of 2003/2004 for final students, in a total of 6 semesters of ICT training.

 

Resources Centre 

     Out of the training timetable, the room also functions as a Resources Centre, open to the community, favouring the educative community. It is provided an informal training here, through a permanent support to the users who, for the most part, have very limited or practically null knowledge of informatics. This support is made through the action of two part-time co-workers, selected by internal contest in HPI, and who help keep the Resources Centre open from 8:30 a.m. to 12:00 and from 2:30 to 7:30 p.m.

     With occupation rates of 100%, the Centre has more than 1200 users (the user card began in June 2001), mostly belonging to the educative community, necessarily with more than 7th term education level. Between June and October 2001 more than 3,600 users frequented the Centre. This huge turnout imposed a computer usage period of 45 minutes.

 

Supports and collaborations

     Moreover, other training courses have been developed, namely the one hold during the “I Biology Teachers National Meeting”, where this Centre actively participated and supported the Portuguese co-operant teachers, mainly in editing and reproducing teaching materials. Owing to the severe deficiencies existing in this country, both in technical and in human terms, the Centre also assists people and institutions, national and foreign, preferably connected to education and culture, like the Instituto Diocesano de Formação (the only school with similar pedagogical level to Portugal), the Portuguese School, Camões Institute and the Portuguese Language Centre, among others.

     The inclusion of the HPI in the telematic network of the Portuguese Language Universities (whose meeting took place in Alcobaça, Portugal, in April 2001, and had the presence of two HPI members) is being carried out with technical and logistical support of the Centre itself.

     Distance education is also under consideration, directed to the HPI students, carried out by the Bragança Polytechnic Institute and with local support of the Competence Centre. Therefore, some experiments on video-conferencing have already been made, between this Institute and the Competence Centre.

     It is also important the partnership with the Federação das Organizações Não Governamentais (Non-Governamental Organizations Federation) of São Tomé and Principe -  FONG-STP  - , where the Competence Centre is responsible for the online publishing of the NGO's features, aims and activities, working in STP.

 

Homepage (www.stome.net)

     As São Tomé and Principe is a country located far from Africa and far from Europe, isolated by men and nature, one of the initial concerns was the publishing of its main features, of some of its secrets and activities.

     The HomePage, edited by the Centre in June 2001, has a generalist information on São Tomé and Principe, supported by a great number of texts and figures, displaying more than 150 photos about several aspects of STP physical and human landscape, both in the present and in the past. It also means to be a vehicle for the spreading of aspects related with education in special, and intends to be in the future a starting point for exchange activities between STP schools and schools from the Portuguese Language Countries Community. It is also being prepared the advertisement of the activities carried through by the Portuguese cooperation, supported by different ministries, working in the field.

  

CONDITIONINGS

     As a country of limited physical and human resources, frequently misused, the obstacles for the implementation of this kind of projects are varied. Without intending to be exhaustive, I refer some of these conditionings:

Educational and technological panorama. São Tomé and Principe faces severe and chronic problems in education. Among other causes, I must stress the insufficiency or inadequacy of the existing infrastructures, the lack of motivation and insufficient specific training of the teaching staff, the lack of manuals and other didactic material, the unsuitability and incoherence between the legally adopted educative system and the real one, the low level of education and the high number of students in the classes. To these factors we can also add the high level of school dropout, the reduced opportunities for techno-professional training and a generalized regression of the education related figures.

     In contrast with this situation, I must stress the High Polytechnic Institute (www.stome.net) oriented to teachers’ initial training at bachelor’s degree, and whose first students finished their course in 2000/2001.

     With this situation in the educational area, in the technological area, with a very reduced number of computers and a great lack of human resources, the needs are huge. And out of the capital town, where these means are concentrated, the rest of the country can be considered practically as a desert in informatics terms.

 The deficient infrastructures at communications level. Although STP is considered as one of the African countries with better telecommunications network, its network is analogical and even the few existing digital lines, are strangled with the connections with foreign countries in the local ISP. This makes it impossible, for instance, to use the videoconferencing devices in distance education, during the day, only possible through satellite connection (I have already begun making downloads at the amazing speed of … 98 bps!). Moreover, the high costs with communications and Internet connections.

 The energy supply and its quality.  Besides the current changes and instability, the electricity cuts are frequent, requiring the use of UPSs and, sometimes, power generators, what makes even more expensive the installation of any technological devices, being these more expensive owing to transportation and commercialisation. (the record of CDs ruined in a simple playback was 7, due to constant energy cuts and when a UPS was not still available).

 Technical support. A simple fault may mean the immobilization of the equipment for a long period, when, and it is the more frequent, the part has to be sent from abroad, though the collaboration of some local representatives. Besides, the ignorance about STP implies a more extended period of time. I can’t help telling a case, which can sound like a joke, though the inconvenience it caused: I had a UPS for all the computers and suddenly, one day, it had a breakdown. As it was within the guarantee and the cause was a fault, the company rapidly offered to substitute the faulty part - the UPS digital board. In spite of the importer calls and faxes, the part was delaying. A month after, this importer, at his own expenses, decided to import the board directly from the representative in Portugal, being the problem solved in two weeks. When the part arrived from the factory in Denmark, we looked at the address on the parcel and understood the reason of that strange delay:

 

Central Industrial Electromecânica, Lda.

Caixa Postal, 137

S. Tomé e Príncipe

Central African Republic

 

     Due to only three unnecessary words in the address, the Competence Centre was partially inoperative for two months. And, unfortunately, this was not the first case and certainly won’t be the last.

  

CONCLUSION

     In a positive perspective, we must stress the great interest and motivation for the Information and Communication Technologies, expressed by the younger generations and the competences revealed by some of them. But they miss the conditions for the development and implementation of this knowledge and allow others to acquire the necessary competences in this area. This is one of the aims of the Competence Centre.

     Though the opportunities of training in this area are scarce, I should put in evidence the activity of a Centre of Competence supported by FCTI, through the program “InforJovem” and the activity of two private companies, one of them responsible for the only “Cybercafé” in STP, opened in the last season of 2001.

     I must also refer the busy newsgroup about STP, a meeting place of ideas and reflections, at http://groups.yahoo.com/group/saotome

     All this represents a set of small evolutions that may mean an advance in the acquisition of the competences for the use of ICT and their implementation.

     The entrance of this country in the oil producers club was very important, because it will help to eliminate some of the present conditionings. I only hope that STP will remain an exception to the rule in what is happening among the Gulf countries. Let us wait and see…

     To conclude, I believe that the use of Information and Communication Technologies, together with the elimination of some relative distances they create, will be an important contribute to make São Tomé and Principe closer to Europe and less far from Africa.


[1] The Archipelago of São Tomé and Príncipe, former Portuguese colony until 1975, is situated in the Guinea Gulf and consists of two main islands, São Tomé and Príncipe, with an area of 857 km2 and distant 280 km from the African coast.  (T.N.)

[2] The Centre was installed in two rooms of the Higher Polytechnic Institute and has 8+1 computers network connected with Internet access, photocopier, printer, fax, scanner, CD-ROM recorder, video projector, digital photography and LCD projector, enabling the digitalisation, printing and photocopy of documents by the users. There is also the facility to consult several titles of educational software, supplied by DAPP - Portuguese Ministry of Education.
