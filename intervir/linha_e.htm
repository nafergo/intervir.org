Editorial Guidelines

    The main idea for the launching of this online journal has to do with the need we feel to create an open space for reflection and sharing of experiences. Like us, many  citizens feel that living in society involves intervention.

         But how To Intervene?

         Starting this adventure is a way of trying to find answers to that question.

    In Education, and as professionals in this area we favour all the approaches centered in the development of the human being, actor and producer of his self and his social environment. The evolution from the information society in which we live to the knowledge society implies a change in the educational culture, it requires pedagogical innovation. Teachers have to leave their traditional posture of sage on the stage and assume the role of mediators of a collective knowledge, without excluded ones, promoting innovative procedures, intervention and participation.

    In the present society, the access to information and the construction of knowledge, communication and sociability present an irrefutable technological dimension. The use of the new information and communication technologies doesn't mean  per si  innovative approaches. However, more and more, they claim to be useful tools in processes of innovation and intervention. The virtual learning communities and cyberdemocracy are already two main concepts in the understanding of a future that rapidly walks towards the present.

    In politics, under a globalisation context to which we can no longer remain indifferent, we consider the equality in accessing information an indispensable condition to the existence of a participative democracy, of a citizenship able to promote new pro-active and mobilising attitudes of new leading positions.

    Thus, with Intervir we try to create a space of shared reflection with all those who, like us, worry about the development of man and community, in educational, technological and political dimensions.
