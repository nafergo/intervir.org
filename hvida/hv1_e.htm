THE NEW TECHNOLOGIES IN PUBLIC BASIC SCHOOLS IN PORTUGAL 

Alexandra Reis [1]

 

     Nowadays, also called Society of Information or Society of Communication,  the new technologies assume a great importance, invading  people’s everyday life and producing huge changes in the organization of our lifes and work.

     However, there is a gap between our schools  and the changes verified in the society. We must be aware that school has lost the importance over the children and youth cultural upbringing and doesn´t have any longer the “monopoly” of knowledge; nowadays, there are many other sources of information, also credible, among which the new technologies of information must be included.

     School has to face the challenge of adapting itself to the new needs of society and, necessarily, keep up with the  development of computer technologies, at the risk of, if not doing so, becoming obsolete, not much attractive and, even worse, not creating citizens able to enter professional training programs which would allow them to enter the active life or to go on with their studies.

     School must “provide all the students with the opportunity of living those learning experiences considered today as fundamental in the varied curricular areas” (DEB:1999).  ICT (Information and Communication Technologies) may constitute another resource to help the achievement of general objectives of Basic Learning, as well as in the development of essential skills that all students have to reach throughout compulsory school.

     The use of ICT in educational activities can be another resource which may contribute to the complete integration of all students (with or without special educative needs), providing them with essential skills to intervene in social, cultural and economic life in an autonomous and effective way and, consequently, encouraging to attenuate social inequalities.

     ICT activities allow students a quicker and deeper understanding of the World where we live, enriching the acquisition of knowledge in different areas. With the use of ICT distances are suppressed and the quantity of available information is huge.

     For some years  in our country, some projects have been being developped with the aim of including computers in the classroom. However, their use is still considered as an innovative practice, perhaps because a great number of basic schools has no computers at all! That use cannot happen owing to fashion purposes or as a comsequence of pressures made by  parents or society in general (that almost always associate the presence of computers in the classroom to improvement in learning eficiency), or only as a mean to attenuate the problems connected to the students performance in the teaching/learning process. A new educational paradigm, which is based in a radical change of the student and teacher roles in School must be underlying: the student will become the builder of his own knowledge, while the teacher will no longer be the carrier of knowledge but the promoter of learning! Then student will have a more central role in the teaching/learning process,  becoming more active, efficient and solid.

     The growing detachment through which students face the teaching/learning process is evident. Computer is a motivation for working and it makes learning easier, and its use has revealed that it is possible to develop new ways of work, involving students into studies in a more direct way and, consequently, improving their performances. We must stress the importance that Project Works may acquire and the great usefulness, in that context, of new technologies.

       “They may be “auxiliary tools” which support the elaboration of texts, statistic data analysis and other information, (…) etc. They may likewise be used as a mean of communication among intervenients and between these and a population engaged in the project.” (PONTE: 1997).

     Through this work dynamics, teachers and students take part in an individual, colective and socially responsible action which stands on democratic values, respecting the diversity of points of view and lived experiences.

     Computer may allow students to discover a new working environment, where they can control their learning processes.” (…) stimulating and varying their cognitive activities and providing them with a more decisive role in their knowledge construction process”. (PONTE: 1997)

     Acording to Bagão (1998), computer can be “a facilitating tool for knowledge, still helping to develop capacities, such as autonomy, solidarity, whereas communication and cooperation are two concepts intimately linked to the computer science world.” The spirit of persistence, the natural motivation of students, his self-esteem (we can “learn” with error), their self-confidence (when they gradually master what they do with the computer) and the skill of solving problems are also developped.

     Child upbringing happens through a series of interactions with the environment where he is inserted (the richer and varied in stimulations and resources, the better will be the child upgrowth).

      “(…) if that environment provides the stimulations, the opportunities and the resources to develop his creativity, to explore and discover, the child will certainly develop intelectual features and ways of thinking which will favour creative, exploratory and inventive thinking; if, beside these stimula, the environment also provides ocasions “to think rigorously, probably testing invented or discovered conjectures and ideas, to test their adequability, the intelectual development will involve, besides elements of creativity, elements of rigour.” (PAPERT: 1980)

     My experience, as a Basic School teacher, allows me to stress the need of an urgent intervention at two levels I consider fundamental: supplying schools with equipment and the continuing training of teachers.

     Even in 1998, the National Council for Education stated, in its opinion about the “Green Book for the Society of Information in Portugal”: “Without the physical means of support, the Society of Information in School is a mere figure of speech and everything else we can add has no meaning” (Opinion # 2/98). Supplying all the schools with computers with Internet access is very urgent. This action must be taken by the Ministery of Education and, eventually, by the local authorities, but also the educative community can and must be mobilized around this objective. Establishing partnerships with the local communities is a strategy to be encouraged.

     We know that it is not enough to put computers in the schools to get  improvement in education, but their absence will separate School from the reality of comunity more end more.

      “In a construtiviste vision, it is not to the computer by itself that can be attributed an “effect” from the cognitive or affective point of view. The context, the interactions between students and teachers, the kind of situations students are exposed to or create, constitute the decisive aspects in the learning process.” (BAGÃO: 1998)

     Although most of the teachers of Basic Schools are not well prepared in this area, they certainly admit the importance and the need of integration of new technologies in the educational context. Using ICT in an educative context implies a change in the teacher’s role: he is no longer the master of knowledge! But his role will always be essential as an inciter of research for knowledge, an assistant in the production of knowledge and in helping students in their formative route.

      “Society observes the transformation of the relationship with knowledge, either by the fast access to information and easy sharing at a world level, or by the new nature and organisation of work.

      In order to keep up with this evolution of society, learning has to bet on new attempts centered in the orientation of individual routes in knowledge, in the easier personalized learning, but also in interactive networks and at distance. The learning of future will have to pass through a change in school culture; through a conscious opening to cyberculture; through teacher/student relationships marked by mutual help; through the involvement of teachers and students in the construction of their own knowledge; through the opening to research and to continuing education and through the development of skills and critical competence.” (MOREIRA: 2001)

     As to the teachers continuing education much more can and must be done to accomplish the integration of computer in educative context, in a full and efficient way. The accomplishment of a training program whose objective is to encourage teachers to use computers in class, through the construction of a project and its implementation in the classroom can be an important step.

      “(...) the existence of continuing education programs that can be implemented in  place of work, designed to make a survey of problems by the interested people themselves and with a supervision which makes easy the construction of their own professional development,  linking  the technology available in the school and the curriculum they teach.”       (SANTOS: 1998).

     In parallel with the development of the several projects in the classrooms, it is important to create a space for training where the knowledge and experience of all the teachers [2] can be used to search for concrete solutions .

       -         How and when to use the computer in the classroom?

       -         What are the objectives of using the computer in the classroom?

     The reflection about these and other issues will result in a truly meaningful training, as developpped in an educational context and with almost immediate results in the pedagogical practice. At the same time that teachers acquire knowledge at informatics level, they give it a meaning because they integrate it in their practice with  students.

     In conclusion, it is important that school ponder on the interaction of ICT with the learning process, as well as on its role in educational context.

      “The new technologies will be able to play a role, either as a work tool, or as a means to discover concepts, or as tools to solve problems. Their use can be highly motivating to students. These technologies develop great educative opportunities. The central issue is to know how they will be understood by the teachers and used by the students.” (PONTE: 1997)
